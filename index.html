I'm pursuing my PhD in Computer Vision and Machine Learning under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Bastian Leibe in the Computer Vision Group at RWTH Aachen University in Aachen, Germany. My research focus is 3D human understanding with robotics application scenarios in mind.

Before joining as a PhD student, I worked with the same group as a master student on crowd counting from images and video. Before that, I designed and analyzed medical image processing algorithms at the Institute of Medical Informatics at RWTH and published papers on eye redness measurement and wound imaging. During my German-language undergrad at the Budapest University of Technology and Economics in Hungary, I specialized in Autonomous and Intelligent Systems. I wrote my thesis at Hungary's National Institute for Strategic Health Research, evaluating neural nets for medical diagnosis classification. In an exchange semester and an internship at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, I focused on robotics, data mining and image processing.
